## What Triggers Capital Gains Tax

CGT applies when you **dispose** of an asset and make a gain. A disposal includes:

- Selling an asset
- Giving it away as a gift
- Transferring it to someone else
- Receiving compensation for an asset (e.g., insurance payout)
- Exchanging one asset for another

Common chargeable assets include shares, investment property, personal possessions worth over the relevant threshold, and business assets.

## Annual Exempt Amount

Each individual receives a tax-free annual exempt amount. Refer to `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/references/2024-25-rates.md` for the exact figure for 2024-25. Gains within this threshold incur no CGT. The allowance cannot be carried forward — use it or lose it each tax year.

## CGT Rates and the Mid-Year Change

For 2024-25, CGT rates changed on **30 October 2024**. This means gains realised before and after that date may be taxed at different rates. Refer to `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/references/2024-25-rates.md` for the specific rates applicable to each period, broken down by asset type and taxpayer band.

CGT rates depend on your **total taxable income**. Add the gain to your income for the year to determine whether it falls within the basic-rate or higher-rate band. Different rates apply to residential property disposals versus other assets — check the rates file for specifics.

## Allowable Costs

Deduct the following from disposal proceeds to calculate the chargeable gain:

- **Original purchase price** (or market value at acquisition if gifted)
- **Improvement costs** that enhanced the asset's value (not maintenance)
- **Incidental costs of acquisition** — solicitor fees, stamp duty, survey costs
- **Incidental costs of disposal** — broker fees, solicitor fees, advertising costs

## Losses

If a disposal produces a loss:

- **Offset against gains in the same tax year** — losses must be used in the current year first, but only to reduce gains down to the annual exempt amount (not below).
- **Carry forward unused losses** indefinitely to set against future gains.
- **Report losses** to HMRC within **4 years** of the end of the tax year in which they arose, or they may be lost.

You cannot create artificial losses. The **bed and breakfast rule** (also called the 30-day rule) prevents selling shares at a loss and rebuying the same shares within 30 days — the disposal is matched to the repurchase, negating the loss.

## Key Reliefs

### Business Asset Disposal Relief (formerly Entrepreneurs' Relief)

Allows qualifying business owners to pay a reduced CGT rate on disposals of business assets, up to a lifetime limit. Refer to `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/references/2024-25-rates.md` for the current rate and lifetime limit. Qualifying conditions include holding at least 5% of shares and voting rights for a minimum of 2 years.

### Investors' Relief

A similar reduced rate for external investors in unlisted trading companies who have held shares for at least 3 years. A separate lifetime limit applies — see the rates file.

### Private Residence Relief

Your main home is usually exempt from CGT. Partial relief may apply if the property was not your main residence throughout ownership, was let out, or has grounds exceeding the permitted area.

## Reporting and Deadlines

Report CGT on the **SA108 Capital Gains Tax summary pages** as part of your Self Assessment. If total disposal proceeds exceed four times the annual exempt amount, you must complete these pages even if no tax is due.

For **UK residential property** disposals completing on or after 6 April 2020, report and pay CGT within **60 days** of completion using the HMRC CGT on UK property service — then also include on your SA return.

The Self Assessment deadline for 2024-25 is **31 January 2026** for online returns. Refer to the rates file for any additional deadline details.
