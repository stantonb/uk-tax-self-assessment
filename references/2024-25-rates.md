# UK Tax Rates, Allowances & Thresholds — 2024/25

Tax year: 6 April 2024 to 5 April 2025

## Income Tax (England & Northern Ireland)

| Band | Taxable income | Rate |
|------|---------------|------|
| Personal Allowance | Up to £12,570 | 0% |
| Basic rate | £12,571 – £50,270 | 20% |
| Higher rate | £50,271 – £125,140 | 40% |
| Additional rate | Over £125,140 | 45% |

- Personal Allowance tapers by £1 for every £2 earned over £100,000
- Personal Allowance is fully lost at £125,140
- Marriage Allowance transfer: £1,260 (max tax saving £252)
- Blind Person's Allowance: £3,070

## National Insurance Contributions

### Class 1 (Employees)
| Threshold | Weekly | Annual |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Primary Threshold (PT) | £242 | £12,570 |
| Upper Earnings Limit (UEL) | £967 | £50,270 |

| Earnings band | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| Up to PT | 0% |
| PT to UEL | 8% |
| Above UEL | 2% |

### Class 2 (Self-Employed)
- Small Profits Threshold: £6,725
- No longer mandatory if profits above SPT (from April 2024)
- Voluntary rate: £3.45/week

### Class 4 (Self-Employed)
| Profits band | Rate |
|-------------|------|
| Up to £12,570 | 0% |
| £12,570 – £50,270 | 6% |
| Above £50,270 | 2% |

## Dividend Tax

- Dividend Allowance: £500
- Basic rate: 8.75%
- Higher rate: 33.75%
- Additional rate: 39.35%

## Savings Income

- Personal Savings Allowance (basic rate): £1,000
- Personal Savings Allowance (higher rate): £500
- Personal Savings Allowance (additional rate): £0
- Starting rate for savings: 0% on first £5,000 (if non-savings income below £17,570)

## Capital Gains Tax

- Annual Exempt Amount: £3,000

### Rates (6 April 2024 – 29 October 2024)
| Asset type | Basic rate | Higher rate |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Most assets | 10% | 20% |
| Residential property | 18% | 28% |

### Rates (30 October 2024 – 5 April 2025)
| Asset type | Basic rate | Higher rate |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Most assets | 18% | 24% |
| Residential property | 18% | 24% |

- Business Asset Disposal Relief: 10% (lifetime limit £1m)
- Investors' Relief: 10% (lifetime limit £10m)

### Crypto/Shares Matching Rules
1. Same-day rule: match disposals with same-day acquisitions first
2. Bed & breakfast rule (30-day): match with acquisitions in next 30 days
3. Section 104 pool: remaining matched against pooled average cost

## Pensions

- Annual Allowance: £60,000
- Money Purchase Annual Allowance (MPAA): £10,000
- Tapered Annual Allowance starts at: £260,000 (adjusted income)
- Minimum tapered Annual Allowance: £10,000
- Lifetime Allowance: Abolished from 6 April 2024
- Lump Sum Allowance: £268,275
- Lump Sum and Death Benefit Allowance: £1,073,100
- Tax relief: at marginal rate on contributions

## ISA

- Annual ISA allowance: £20,000
- Lifetime ISA allowance: £4,000 (within £20,000 total)
- Junior ISA allowance: £9,000
- All growth and income within ISA is tax-free

## Share Incentive Plan (SIP)

- Free Shares: up to £3,600/year from employer
- Partnership Shares: up to £1,800/year or 10% of salary (whichever is lower)
- Matching Shares: up to 2:1 ratio on partnership shares
- Tax-free if held in plan for 5+ years
- No income tax or NI on money used to buy partnership shares
- No CGT if sold directly from the plan

## High Income Child Benefit Charge (HICBC)

- Threshold: £60,000 (from 6 April 2024, previously £50,000)
- Full charge at: £80,000
- Charge rate: 1% of child benefit for every £200 over £60,000
- Based on adjusted net income of higher-earning partner
- Child Benefit rates (weekly):
  - Eldest/only child: £25.60
  - Each additional child: £16.95

## Self Assessment Key Dates

- 6 April 2024: Tax year starts
- 5 April 2025: Tax year ends
- 5 October 2025: Register for SA if new (first return)
- 31 October 2025: Paper return deadline
- 31 January 2026: Online return deadline + payment deadline
- 31 July 2026: Second payment on account deadline

## Charitable Giving

- Gift Aid: charity reclaims 25p for every £1 donated
- Higher/additional rate taxpayers can claim difference via SA
- Payroll giving: donations from gross pay (no tax)

## Working From Home

- Flat rate allowance: £6/week (£312/year) — no evidence required
- Actual costs: proportion of household bills if working from home regularly
- Only available if employer requires home working (not by choice)

## Allowable Business Expenses (Self-Employed / Side Business)

- Must be "wholly and exclusively" for business purposes
- Mixed-use items: claim business proportion only
- Record-keeping: keep records for at least 5 years after 31 January submission deadline
- Cash basis available if turnover under £150,000
- Simplified expenses available for home office, vehicles, living at business premises
