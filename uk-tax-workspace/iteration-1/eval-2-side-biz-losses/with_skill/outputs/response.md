# UK Tax Guidance: Nurse with Online Tutoring Side Business — 2024/25

> **DISCLAIMER**: This guidance provides general tax information only. It is NOT professional tax advice. Tax situations vary — always verify with HMRC or a qualified accountant before making decisions.

---

## Your Situation at a Glance

- Employment: Nurse, earning £35,000 (taxed via PAYE)
- Side business: Online tutoring, started September 2024
- Revenue: £3,200
- Expenses claimed: £5,500 (laptop, webcam, software subscriptions, advertising)
- Apparent position: A trading loss of £2,300 before any housing costs

This is a common situation for people who start a side business partway through the year with significant upfront equipment costs. The good news is that the loss can potentially work in your favour. Let's work through each part.

---

## 1. Do You Need to Register and File a Self Assessment Return?

Yes. Your tutoring revenue of £3,200 is above the £1,000 trading allowance threshold. This means you are required to:

1. **Register as self-employed with HMRC** — by 5 October 2025 (5 October following the end of the 2024/25 tax year in which your trading started).
2. **File a Self Assessment tax return** — online deadline is 31 January 2026; paper deadline is 31 October 2025.

Even if no tax is owed (because you have made a loss), registering and filing is required — and in your case, it is also beneficial because it lets you formally claim loss relief.

**How to register**: go to gov.uk and search "register for Self Assessment." Follow the self-employed path. HMRC will post you a Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) within approximately 10 working days. Keep it safe.

---

## 2. Are Your Expenses Allowable? (The £5,500 You Spent)

The core rule for self-employed expenses is that they must be incurred "wholly and exclusively" for the purposes of the trade. Here is how your specific costs are treated:

### Laptop and Webcam (Capital Equipment)

These are capital items — they have lasting value beyond a single tax year. They are not claimed as a straightforward running expense. Instead, they go through **capital allowances**, specifically the **Annual Investment Allowance (AIA)**, which gives you 100% relief in the year of purchase.

**However**, a laptop is almost certainly a mixed-use item — used for both tutoring and personal purposes. You can only claim the **business proportion**. If you estimate you use it 70% for tutoring, you claim 70% of the cost.

If you are using the cash basis (which is available and simpler for small side businesses with turnover under £150,000), most plant and machinery expenditure — including computers — is deductible when paid, so the full business proportion is simply included in your expenses for the year. Cash basis is the default for small businesses and is the simpler approach.

**Practical example**: Laptop costs £1,200. You use it 70% for tutoring. Deductible amount: £840.

### Software Subscriptions

Fully allowable if wholly used for the tutoring business (e.g., video conferencing tools, tutoring platforms, accounting software). If a subscription is partly personal, claim only the business proportion.

### Advertising

Fully allowable — marketing and advertising costs directly related to the business are a standard allowable expense.

### Webcam

Allowable as business equipment (same rules as the laptop — claim the business proportion under cash basis or AIA under accruals).

**Summary**: Your £5,500 of spending is likely to be substantially allowable, but you should review items used for both personal and business purposes and claim only the business-use proportion. The net effect may reduce the full deductible amount somewhat from the £5,500 headline figure, but the majority should qualify.

## 3. Can You Claim Rent and Bills?

This is one of the most commonly misunderstood areas. The answer is: **yes, a proportion — but not the full amount, and not automatically.**

### You Have Two Options. You Must Choose One and Stick to It.

#### Option A: Flat Rate (Simplified Expenses)

HMRC provides fixed monthly amounts based on hours you work from home on your tutoring business per month:

| Hours worked from home per month | Monthly flat rate |
|----------------------------------|-------------------|
| 25–50 hours                      | £10               |
| 51–100 hours                     | £18               |
| 101+ hours                       | £26               |

You started in September 2024, so you have approximately 7 months in the tax year (September 2024 to April 2025). If you work more than 101 hours per month on tutoring from home, you could claim £26 x 7 = £182.

**Advantages**: no receipts needed for household bills; very simple; no risk of complications.

**Disadvantage**: the amount is small relative to actual housing costs.

#### Option B: Actual Costs (Proportionate Method)

Calculate a reasonable business proportion of your actual household running costs. This requires two steps:

**Step 1 — Room proportion**: how many rooms does your flat have (excluding bathrooms, kitchens, hallways)? If you use one room out of four for tutoring, the room proportion is 25%.

**Step 2 — Time proportion**: that room is probably not used exclusively for tutoring. If you use it for business 40 hours out of 168 hours in a week, the time proportion is approximately 24%.

**Combined proportion**: 25% x 24% = 6% of your housing costs.

**Costs you can then apply this proportion to:**
- Rent
- Gas and electricity
- Water rates
- Broadband (often a higher proportion is justified if the business relies heavily on internet)
- Home insurance (contents)
- Council tax

**Example calculation** (illustrative):
- Monthly rent: £1,200 → annual rent: £14,400
- Annual utilities/broadband/insurance: £2,400
- Total housing costs: £16,800
- At 6% business proportion: £1,008 claimable

This is modest but real, and meaningfully larger than the flat rate.

### Important: Check Your Tenancy Agreement

Before claiming rent, check your lease. Many residential tenancy agreements restrict or prohibit business use of the property. Running a tutoring business from your flat without your landlord's permission could breach the lease. It is recommended to obtain written consent from your landlord.

### Reminder: You Cannot Mix the Two Methods

If you use the flat rate, you cannot also claim actual household costs. Choose one method for the entire tax year.

## 4. What Is Your Trading Loss and What Can You Do With It?

Let us work through the numbers at a high level.

**Revenue**: £3,200
**Allowable expenses** (approximate, before housing costs): likely somewhere in the range of £4,000–£5,000 after applying business-use proportions to mixed-use items.
**Housing costs** (if claiming actual costs): say £1,000 (illustrative).

This produces a **trading loss** — likely somewhere between £1,800 and £2,800 depending on exact apportionment.

You have several options for what to do with this loss:

### Option 1: Carry Forward Against Future Tutoring Profits

The loss is carried forward and set against profits from your tutoring business in future tax years. There is no time limit. This is the default and simplest option.

**Best if**: you expect the tutoring business to become profitable soon and do not need immediate tax relief.

### Option 2: Sideways Relief — Offset Against Your Employment Income (Most Valuable Option Here)

You can offset the trading loss against **other income in the same tax year** — including your nursing salary. This is potentially very valuable.

**How it works with your numbers**:
- Nursing salary: £35,000
- Less Personal Allowance: £12,570
- Taxable employment income: £22,430
- Trading loss (say £2,300): reduces taxable income to £20,130
- Tax saving: approximately £460 at the 20% basic rate (£2,300 x 20%)

This means HMRC effectively reimburses you for part of the income tax you paid through PAYE on your nursing salary.

**Restrictions to be aware of**:
- HMRC must be satisfied your tutoring business is a **genuine trade conducted on a commercial basis with a reasonable expectation of profit** — not a hobby. Online tutoring is a well-recognised trade and you clearly have revenue (£3,200 in your first half-year), which strongly supports this.
- Sideways relief must be claimed within 12 months of the 31 January filing deadline for the relevant tax year (so by 31 January 2027 for 2024/25).
- **Cash basis restriction**: if you use the cash basis, sideways relief and carry-back are generally not available. If you want to claim sideways relief, you may need to use the accruals basis instead. This is an important decision — consider discussing with an accountant.

### Option 3: Early Years Loss Relief (Carry Back Three Years)

Because your tutoring business started in 2024/25, you are in the **first four tax years** of a new trade. This means losses can be carried back against your general income from the **three tax years before 2024/25** (i.e., back to 2021/22), earliest year first.

This could be valuable if you were in a higher tax band in earlier years, or simply to get a tax refund from prior years. HMRC would repay the tax from those earlier years.

**Best if**: you had significant income in earlier years, or you would prefer a cash refund from prior years rather than a current-year reduction.

### Which Option to Choose?

For most people in your position, **sideways relief** (Option 2) is the most immediately useful — it reduces your current year's tax bill (or generates a refund) and is simpler to administer than carry-back. However, the cash basis restriction is important: if you want sideways relief, you will need to opt out of cash basis and use accruals accounting for this year.

Given the relatively modest loss amount, the simplest path may be to carry the loss forward (Option 1) and use it against future tutoring profits — especially if the business is expected to grow and become profitable.

## 5. National Insurance

As a self-employed person alongside your employment, you are subject to additional NI:

- **Class 1 NI** is already being deducted from your nursing salary through PAYE — this is unaffected.
- **Class 2 NI**: from April 2024, Class 2 NI is no longer mandatory if profits are above the Small Profits Threshold (£6,725). Because you have made a **loss**, your profits are below this threshold. You may choose to pay **voluntarily at £3.45/week** to protect your State Pension entitlement — consider whether your total NI record is sufficient without it.
- **Class 4 NI**: charged on self-employment profits. Because your tutoring business has made a loss, there are no Class 4 NI liabilities.

## 6. Your Tax Return — What to Include

When completing your Self Assessment return, you will need:

- **Employment pages**: your P60 from the NHS/employer showing £35,000 salary and tax paid.
- **Self-employment pages (SA103)**: your tutoring income (£3,200), your allowable expenses broken down by category, any capital allowances claimed, your WFH costs, and your resulting profit or loss.
- **Loss relief election**: if claiming sideways relief, tick the relevant box in the self-employment pages to offset the loss against other income.

## 7. Action Checklist

1. **Register as self-employed** with HMRC by 5 October 2025 if you have not already done so. Do this at gov.uk.

2. **Check your tenancy agreement** before claiming rent costs. Get written landlord consent if needed.

3. **Gather your records** now:
   - All income received from tutoring (invoices, bank receipts)
   - Receipts for the laptop, webcam, software, advertising
   - Utility bills, rent receipts for the period September 2024 – April 2025
   - Your P60 from your employer

4. **Decide on your accounting method**: cash basis (simpler, but restricts loss relief to carry forward only) or accruals (more complex, but allows sideways loss relief against your nursing salary).

5. **Decide on your WFH method**: flat rate (simple, small amount) or actual costs (more calculation, potentially worthwhile).

6. **File your Self Assessment return** by 31 January 2026 (online) for the 2024/25 tax year.

7. **Consider consulting an accountant** for this first year — the decision between cash basis and accruals, and whether to claim sideways relief, early years carry-back, or carry forward, can have meaningful financial consequences and an accountant can help you optimise the choice.

## Summary

Your tutoring business has made a genuine trading loss in its first (part) year, which is very common when there are upfront equipment costs. You cannot claim the full rent — but you can claim a reasonable business proportion using either the flat rate or actual cost method. Your expenses (laptop, webcam, software, advertising) are largely allowable, subject to apportioning mixed-use items.

The loss — likely around £1,800–£2,800 — can be used to reduce your income tax bill on your nursing salary through sideways relief (potentially saving you £360–£560 in tax), provided you use accruals accounting rather than cash basis, and HMRC is satisfied the business is a genuine commercial venture. Alternatively, carry it forward to offset future tutoring profits.

Register, file your return, keep your records, and consider taking professional advice to make the most of the loss relief options available to you.
