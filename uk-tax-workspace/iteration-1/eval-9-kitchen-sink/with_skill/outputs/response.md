ANI determines your personal allowance and HICBC position. It is calculated as:

## Step 4: Personal Allowance Taper

Your ANI is **£101,000** — above the £100,000 threshold.

The personal allowance tapers by £1 for every £2 over £100,000:

- ANI over threshold: £101,000 - £100,000 = £1,000
- Taper: £1,000 / 2 = £500
- Reduced personal allowance: £12,570 - £500 = **£12,070**

You lose £500 of your personal allowance. The effective marginal rate in the £100,000-£125,140 band is approximately **60%** (higher rate tax on the income + the tax value of the lost allowance). This is sometimes called the "£100k trap."

**Key planning point**: You are only £1,000 above the £100,000 threshold after the Gift Aid deduction. An additional pension contribution of just £1,000 gross (or slightly above to be safe) into a relief-at-source arrangement would bring your ANI back down to £100,000 and fully restore your personal allowance, saving you around £500 in additional tax at effectively 60% marginal relief.

## Step 6: Rental Income and the Mortgage Interest Restriction

This is one of the more complicated parts of your position.

You have:
- Rental profit after expenses (excluding mortgage interest): **£6,200**
- Mortgage interest: **£4,800**

Under current rules, **mortgage interest cannot be deducted from rental profits**. Instead, you receive a **basic rate tax credit** on the finance costs:

- Tax credit: 20% × £4,800 = **£960**

This credit is offset against your final income tax bill. However, your rental profit of £6,200 is taxed in full at 40% (as a higher rate taxpayer), costing:

- Tax on rental profit: 40% × £6,200 = **£2,480**
- Less mortgage interest tax credit: -£960
- **Net tax cost on rental income: £1,520**

Effectively you're paying 24.5% of your gross rental receipts (profit + mortgage interest = £11,000) in tax. This is the real-world impact of the mortgage interest restriction on higher rate taxpayers. Before the restriction was introduced, you would have deducted interest from profit and only paid tax on the net £6,200 less interest = £1,400, saving considerably more.

**The mortgage interest also inflates your reported income**, which is one reason your non-savings income total is what it is — the full £6,200 profit counts, not a net figure after interest.

## Step 8: Capital Gains Tax — Ethereum and Token Swaps

### Ethereum sale — £7,200 gain

**Annual Exempt Amount**: £3,000 for 2024/25.

Taxable gain: £7,200 - £3,000 = **£4,200**

Your total income (£103,500) well exceeds the higher rate threshold (£50,270), so the entire gain is taxed at the **higher rate**.

**CGT rate depends on disposal date:**

- Before 30 October 2024: 20% on crypto/assets (not residential property)
- From 30 October 2024: 24% on crypto/assets

If the Ethereum was sold before 30 October 2024: CGT = 20% × £4,200 = **£840**
If sold after 30 October 2024: CGT = 24% × £4,200 = **£1,008**

If the disposal straddled the date change, apportion accordingly.

### Token-to-token swaps — THIS IS CRITICAL

Every token-to-token swap is a **taxable disposal** under HMRC rules. When you swapped "some other tokens around," each swap triggered a CGT calculation at that moment, using the GBP market value at the time of the swap as both the disposal proceeds and the acquisition cost for the new tokens.

You need to:
1. List every swap with dates and token amounts
2. Identify GBP market values at the time of each disposal
3. Apply the share pooling rules (same-day, 30-day bed and breakfast, then Section 104 pool) for each token type
4. Calculate gain or loss on each disposal
5. Aggregate with the Ethereum gain and apply the annual exempt amount across all crypto gains

Any gains from swaps will likely also be subject to the higher rate (20% or 24% depending on timing). Any losses from swaps can be offset against gains, potentially reducing the Ethereum tax.

**Record-keeping requirement**: You must maintain detailed records of every transaction — date, amount in tokens, GBP value at the time, exchange records, wallet addresses. HMRC has data-sharing arrangements with UK exchanges. Keep records for at least 5 years after the 31 January filing deadline.

**Reporting**: Use the **SA108 Capital Gains Tax summary pages**. If your total disposal proceeds across all crypto exceed four times the annual exempt amount (4 × £3,000 = £12,000), you must complete SA108 even if no tax is due.

**2. Increase salary sacrifice if possible**

On income above £50,270, the combined saving is 42% (40% + 2%). The employer also saves employer NI, so you may be able to negotiate some of that saving being added to your pension.

Gather all your crypto transaction records now, before the 31 January 2026 deadline. If any token swaps generated losses, these can offset your Ethereum gain and reduce CGT. The maths on this can be compelling — every £1 of gross pension contribution above £100,000 of income effectively costs you only 40p after tax relief (and even less when HICBC recovery is factored in).
