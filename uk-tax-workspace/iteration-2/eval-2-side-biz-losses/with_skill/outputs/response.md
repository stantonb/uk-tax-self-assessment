You have two income sources for the 2024/25 tax year (6 April 2024 to 5 April 2025):

- **Self-employment**: Online tutoring business, started September 2024. Revenue of £3,200, expenses of approximately £5,500, creating a trading loss before any housing cost claims.

## 1. Your Business Expenses: What Is Allowable

Your £5,500 spend breaks down into items that are broadly allowable as business expenses, subject to some important nuances.

### Laptop — Capital Expenditure

A laptop is a capital item (it has lasting value beyond a single tax year). It is **not** claimed as a straightforward revenue expense. Instead, you claim it through **capital allowances**.

The good news: under the **Annual Investment Allowance (AIA)**, you can claim 100% of the cost in the year of purchase for qualifying plant and machinery. A laptop used for tutoring qualifies.

If your tutoring business also uses the **cash basis** of accounting (which most new small businesses do — see section 3 below), capital expenditure on equipment is generally deductible when paid, which has the same practical effect.

**Important caveat on mixed use**: if you use the laptop for personal purposes as well as business, you can only claim the **business proportion**. For example, if 80% of use is tutoring and 20% is personal, you claim 80% of the cost. Be prepared to justify this split if HMRC enquires.

### Webcam

Same treatment as the laptop — a capital item, deductible via capital allowances or under cash basis. Apply any mixed-use apportionment.

### Software Subscriptions

Recurring software costs (e.g., video conferencing tools, teaching platforms, lesson planning apps) are revenue expenses, deductible in full if used exclusively for the business. If a subscription is partly personal (e.g., a general productivity tool), claim only the business proportion.

### Advertising

Fully allowable as a revenue expense — marketing and advertising costs directly related to your tutoring business are deductible with no restrictions.

### Summary

All four categories of spend are in principle allowable (or a business proportion is allowable). The total deductible amount depends on confirming the business/personal split for the laptop and webcam. Assuming the expenses are broadly as stated, you are likely sitting on a genuine trading loss.

## 2. Can You Claim Rent and Bills?

You work from your rented flat. Yes, you can claim a proportion of housing costs — but **not** all of your rent and not all of your bills. There are two methods; you must choose one for the tax year and apply it consistently.

### Option A: Flat Rate (Simplified Expenses) — Simpler

HMRC provides fixed monthly deductions based on hours worked from home for the business each month:

| Hours worked from home per month | Monthly flat rate |
|----------------------------------|-------------------|
| 25 to 50 hours                   | £10               |
| 51 to 100 hours                  | £18               |
| 101 or more hours                | £26               |

You started in September 2024, so you have approximately 7 months in the tax year (September 2024 to April 2025). If you worked, say, 51–100 hours per month on tutoring, you could claim 7 × £18 = **£126**. At the top band (101+ hours), that is 7 × £26 = **£182**.

**Advantages**: no need to gather or apportion utility bills; no risk of overcomplicating the return.
**Disadvantages**: the flat rate is modest and will be considerably less than the actual proportion of your housing costs.

### Option B: Actual Costs — More Detailed, Potentially Higher

You calculate the genuine business share of your housing costs using two steps:

**Step 1 — Room proportion**: divide the number of rooms used for business by the total usable rooms (exclude bathrooms, kitchen, hallways). For example, if you use one room out of 4 usable rooms, that is 25%.

**Step 2 — Time proportion**: if the room is also used personally (e.g., it doubles as your living room or bedroom), reduce by the fraction of time used for business. For example, if you tutor for 40 hours a week in a room that is occupied for roughly 70 hours a week (waking hours), the time proportion is approximately 57%. Combined: 25% × 57% ≈ 14%.

Apply that combined percentage to your actual annual costs:

- Rent
- Council tax
- Gas and electricity
- Water
- Broadband and phone line (or a higher proportion if your internet is central to tutoring)
- Building/contents insurance

**Example calculation** (illustrative):

| Annual cost | Combined proportion | Claimable |
|-------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Rent: £9,600 | 14% | £1,344 |
| Utilities (gas/electric/water): £1,200 | 14% | £168 |
| Council tax: £1,500 | 14% | £210 |
| Broadband: £360 | 25% (heavier business use) | £90 |
| **Total** | | **~£1,812** |

These are illustrative figures — use your actual numbers.

**One important practical point — check your tenancy agreement**: many residential leases restrict or prohibit business use of the property. Before claiming rent proportions, check whether running a business from the flat is permitted. If not, obtain written consent from your landlord. This is both a tax records matter and a legal one.

**The flat rate and actual costs cannot be combined** — choose one method for the entire tax year.

## 3. The Critical Issue: Cash Basis vs Accruals and Sideways Loss Relief

This is the most important decision you face, and it is easy to get wrong.

### The Cash Basis Restriction on Loss Relief

Most new small businesses default to the **cash basis** of accounting — recording income when received and expenses when paid. It is simpler and HMRC allows it for turnover under £150,000.

**However**: under the cash basis, trading losses can generally **only be carried forward** against future profits of the same trade. You **cannot** use cash basis losses to offset your nursing salary (sideways relief) or carry them back to previous years.

### Sideways Relief and Accruals Basis

If you want to offset the tutoring loss against your £35,000 nursing income in the **same tax year**, you must use **accruals basis** accounting instead. Under accruals:

- You can claim **sideways relief** — setting the trading loss against your other income in 2024/25.
- Your total taxable income is reduced by the amount of the loss.
- This means HMRC refunds overpaid PAYE tax at your marginal rate of 20% (basic rate).

**Illustrative tax saving from sideways relief**:

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Employment income | £35,000 |
| Less: trading loss (before housing costs) | (£2,300) |
| Reduced taxable income | £32,700 |
| Tax saving at 20% basic rate | **~£460** |

If you also claim a proportion of housing costs as business expenses, the loss (and therefore the saving) increases further.

### Which Basis to Choose?

For 2024/25 specifically, **accruals basis is likely worth choosing** if you want to benefit from sideways loss relief against your PAYE income. The tax saving at 20% on the losses could be meaningful, especially once housing costs are included.

However, accruals basis is slightly more complex. You will need to account for any amounts owed to you or by you at year end that have not yet been paid — though for a small tutoring business, this is unlikely to be complicated.

### Early Years Loss Relief — Another Option

Because 2024/25 is the **first tax year** of your trade, you may also be eligible for **early years loss relief**. This allows losses from the first four years of trading to be carried back against your general income from the **three preceding tax years** (earliest first). This could produce a refund of tax paid in 2021/22, 2022/23, or 2023/24 if you paid income tax in those years.

Whether this is more or less beneficial than sideways relief depends on your income and tax rates in those earlier years. Both options cannot be claimed simultaneously for the same loss.

### Commercial Basis Requirement

For sideways relief to be available, your tutoring business must be conducted on a **commercial basis with a genuine expectation of profit**. A tutoring business offering real educational services, charging market rates, and building a client base will clearly satisfy this test. You should not have any difficulty here — but keep records demonstrating the business is run as a genuine enterprise (rate cards, client communications, bookings, accounts).

The sideways relief cap is £25,000 per year, which is far above your current loss level, so no restriction applies here.

## 4. Your Tax Position for 2024/25

### Employment Income

Your £35,000 salary is taxed through PAYE throughout the year. Your tax code (likely 1257L) applies the standard Personal Allowance of £12,570, leaving £22,430 taxable at 20% = approximately **£4,486 income tax**. You also pay Class 1 NI at 8% on earnings between £12,570 and £35,000 (£22,430) = approximately **£1,794 NI**.

These amounts will have been deducted by your employer and are not additional obligations — they feed into your overall picture.

### Self-Employment: No Tax or NI Due on the Business Itself

Since the business made a loss, there are no self-employment profits to tax and no Class 4 NI to pay. In fact, the loss provides relief.

### Impact of Sideways Loss Relief (Accruals Basis)

If you claim sideways relief, HMRC will recalculate your total income tax liability for 2024/25 taking into account the reduced total income. Any overpaid PAYE will be repaid after you file your Self Assessment return.

## 5. Registration and Filing Obligations

### Register by 5 October 2025

You started trading in September 2024 (within the 2024/25 tax year). You must register with HMRC as self-employed by **5 October 2025**. Do not miss this deadline — even if you owe no tax, late registration can result in penalties.

Register online at gov.uk — search "register for Self Assessment." HMRC will post you a **Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR)**, typically within 10 working days.

### File Your Self Assessment Return by 31 January 2026

The online return for 2024/25 is due by **31 January 2026**. You will complete:
- SA100 (main return)
- SA102 (employment pages — using figures from your P60)
- SA103S (short self-employment pages, unless your turnover is above the threshold for the full SA103F)

The return will ask you to declare whether you are using cash basis or accruals — this is where you make your accounting choice for the year.

### No VAT Obligation

Your tutoring turnover of £3,200 is far below the £90,000 VAT registration threshold. No VAT action is needed.

### Making Tax Digital (MTD)

MTD for Income Tax becomes mandatory from April 2026 only for those with gross self-employment or rental income over £50,000. You are not affected for now.

### Payments on Account

If your net tax bill (after PAYE) exceeds £1,000, HMRC would normally require payments on account towards next year. With a loss this year, this is unlikely to be triggered — the return will show no extra tax owing and potentially a repayment.

## 6. Practical Steps — What to Do Now

1. **Decide on accounting basis** — accruals basis if you want sideways relief to offset nursing income; cash basis if you are content to carry the loss forward against future tutoring profits.

2. **Check your tenancy agreement** for any restriction on business use. If uncertain, write to your landlord for written permission before claiming housing costs.

3. **Calculate your actual housing cost proportion** using room count and time spent tutoring, and compare it to the flat rate to determine which gives the better result.

4. **Document the business/personal split** for the laptop and webcam. Note your rationale in writing and keep it with your records.

5. **Register with HMRC** at gov.uk as self-employed by 5 October 2025.

6. **Collect all records**: your P60 from the NHS/employer, all invoices to tutoring clients, all receipts for the laptop, webcam, software, and advertising, utility bills and rent statements.

7. **File your Self Assessment return** online by 31 January 2026. Claim the appropriate loss relief (sideways or carry forward) on the SA103 self-employment pages.

8. **Consider state pension**: Class 2 NI is no longer mandatory if profits are above the Small Profits Threshold of £6,725, but since you have a loss this year, you are below it. Paying voluntary Class 2 NI (£3.45/week) would protect a state pension qualifying year. Given you are employed and paying Class 1 NI through your nursing job, you will already be accruing a state pension qualifying year from employment — so voluntary Class 2 is not needed in 2024/25.

## 7. Record-Keeping

Keep all records for at least **5 years after the 31 January 2026 submission deadline** (i.e., until at least January 2031). If you carry the loss forward, retain records until 5 years after the return in which the loss is finally used.

Records to keep:
- All tutoring invoices and payment receipts
- Laptop, webcam, software, and advertising receipts
- Rent receipts or tenancy agreement
- Utility bills (annual summaries are sufficient)
- Council tax bills
- A written note of your room/time apportionment calculation
- Your P60 from your employer
