# UK Savings Interest and Self Assessment: Do You Need to Declare It?

## Your Situation at a Glance

You have the following income to consider:

| Source | Amount |
|---|---|
| Employment income | £48,000 |
| Marcus savings interest | £870 |
| Nationwide savings interest | £320 |
| NS&I Income Bonds interest | £410 |
| Premium Bond winnings | £500 |
| **Total interest (excluding Premium Bonds)** | **£1,600** |

---

## The Short Answer

**Yes, you likely need to declare your savings interest via Self Assessment**, because your total interest from bank and savings accounts (£1,600) exceeds your Personal Savings Allowance (PSA). However, your Premium Bond winnings are completely tax-free and do not need to be declared at all.

---

## Why Banks Do NOT Sort Out the Tax Automatically

You are right that banks now pay interest **gross** (without deducting tax at source) — this changed back in April 2016. Before that, basic rate tax was automatically withheld. However, paying gross does **not** mean tax is handled for you. What actually happens is:

- HMRC attempts to collect tax on **small amounts** of interest by adjusting your PAYE tax code (so it comes out of your salary).
- But this only works if the amount is relatively modest and HMRC knows about it.
- If your interest **exceeds your Personal Savings Allowance**, you are responsible for ensuring the tax is paid — either via a PAYE coding adjustment or Self Assessment.

Banks do report interest to HMRC, so HMRC will generally be aware of it. But it is ultimately **your responsibility** to ensure you pay the correct tax.

---

## The Personal Savings Allowance (PSA)

Your PSA depends on your Income Tax band:

| Tax Band | Annual Income | PSA |
|---|---|---|
| Basic rate (20%) | Up to £50,270 | £1,000 |
| Higher rate (40%) | £50,271 – £125,140 | £500 |
| Additional rate (45%) | Over £125,140 | £0 |

Your employment income is £48,000. After accounting for the standard Personal Allowance of £12,570, your taxable income from employment is £35,430 — which falls entirely within the basic rate band. This means your PSA is **£1,000**.

---

## How Your Interest Is Taxed

Your total taxable savings interest is:

- Marcus: £870
- Nationwide: £320
- NS&I Income Bonds: £410
- **Total: £1,600**

Your PSA covers the first £1,000, leaving **£600 of interest that is taxable**.

At the basic rate of 20%, you would owe **£120 in tax** on that excess £600.

Note: NS&I Income Bonds interest is treated exactly the same as bank interest for tax purposes — it is taxable savings income, not exempt.

---

## Premium Bonds: Completely Tax-Free

The £500 you won on Premium Bonds is **entirely exempt from Income Tax**. Premium Bond prizes are not interest in the legal sense — they are tax-free winnings. You do **not** need to declare this amount anywhere on a tax return.

---

## Do You Need to Complete a Self Assessment Return?

HMRC may be able to collect the tax owed (£120) by adjusting your PAYE tax code, without you needing to complete a full Self Assessment return. However, you are **required to register for Self Assessment** if your untaxed income (including savings interest above your PSA) exceeds **£2,500 in the tax year**. In your case, the excess is £600, which is below that threshold.

That said, you should still **notify HMRC** of the additional interest income if it is not already reflected in your tax code. You can do this:

1. **Online via your Personal Tax Account** at gov.uk — this is the simplest route. HMRC will adjust your tax code to collect the underpayment.
2. **By calling HMRC** (0300 200 3300) to update them.
3. **Via Self Assessment** if you are already registered, or if HMRC asks you to complete a return.

If HMRC has already adjusted your tax code based on information received from your banks, you may find the tax is already being collected and no further action is required. Check your tax code notice (P2) or your Personal Tax Account to confirm.

---

## Summary of Actions

- **Premium Bonds (£500):** No action needed — fully tax-free.
- **Savings interest (£1,600 total):** You have £600 above your PSA, on which you owe £120 tax.
- **Check your PAYE tax code** — if HMRC has already factored in your interest income, it may already be accounted for.
- **If not already covered:** Log in to your Personal Tax Account at gov.uk and update HMRC with your savings interest figures, or register for Self Assessment if directed to do so.
- **You do not need to register for Self Assessment solely because of this interest**, as the untaxed amount (£600) is below the £2,500 Self Assessment threshold for untaxed income — but you must ensure the tax is paid one way or another.
