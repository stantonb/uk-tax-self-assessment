## 4. Capital Gains Tax — Bitcoin

### Bitcoin gain: £4,500

Cryptocurrency is treated as a **capital asset** by HMRC. Each disposal (sale, trade, or use of crypto to purchase goods) is a taxable event.

#### Annual Exempt Amount (AEA)
For 2024/25, the Capital Gains Tax Annual Exempt Amount is **£3,000**.

#### Taxable Gain
- Total gain: £4,500
- Less AEA: £3,000
- **Taxable gain: £1,500**

#### CGT Rate
Because your salary of £72,000 already places you well into the higher-rate band, your capital gains are taxed at the **higher CGT rate**:

- For assets other than residential property: **20%** for higher/additional rate taxpayers

> **Note:** From 30 October 2024 (Autumn Budget 2024), the CGT rates on non-property assets increased. The rate for higher-rate taxpayers rose from 20% to **24%** for disposals made on or after 30 October 2024. If your Bitcoin disposal was before 30 October 2024, the rate is 20%; if on or after that date, it is 24%.

**CGT owed:**
- If disposal before 30 Oct 2024: £1,500 × 20% = **£300**
- If disposal on/after 30 Oct 2024: £1,500 × 24% = **£360**

You will need to **report this on a Self Assessment tax return** and pay any CGT owed by **31 January 2026**.

## 5. Shares from Company Share Incentive Plan (SIP)

### SIP — 3 Years Holding

Share Incentive Plans are a HMRC-approved share scheme. The tax treatment depends on **how long you have held the shares** and **the type of shares** (Free Shares, Partnership Shares, Matching Shares, or Dividend Shares).

#### The 5-Year Rule
- Shares held in a SIP for **5 or more years** are completely free of Income Tax and NIC on withdrawal.
- Shares held for **3 to 5 years**: You will pay **Income Tax and NIC on the lower of (a) the market value at withdrawal or (b) the market value at acquisition** (for Partnership/Matching shares). For Free Shares, tax is on the lower of the original award value or current market value.
- Shares held for **less than 3 years**: Full Income Tax and NIC on the market value at the date of withdrawal.

Since you have held the SIP shares for **3 years**, you fall into the **3–5 year bracket**.

#### Income Tax and NIC on SIP Withdrawal
When you withdraw the shares from the SIP plan (or instruct the trustees to sell), the taxable amount subject to Income Tax and NIC will be:

- **The lower of:** the market value when you acquired them OR the market value when you withdraw/sell them.

Because you are a higher-rate taxpayer:
- Income Tax on the SIP gain subject to charge: **40%**
- Employee NIC on the SIP gain: **2%** (above the Upper Earnings Limit, since your salary is already £72,000)

**Important:** You should check with your employer's SIP administrator for the exact figures. They will typically handle the Income Tax and NIC deduction through PAYE when you sell or withdraw the shares.

#### Capital Gains Tax on SIP Shares
After the shares leave the SIP trust (i.e., are transferred to you), your CGT base cost is established as the **market value at the date of withdrawal from the plan** (not the original price). Any subsequent gain between withdrawal value and eventual sale price would be subject to CGT.

If you sell **immediately upon withdrawal**, there is generally **no additional CGT gain** to report, since the base cost equals the sale price.

#### Recommendation
- Consider waiting until you have held the shares for **5 years** to benefit from full Income Tax and NIC exemption on the SIP shares. At 3 years, you will face a tax charge on the lower of acquisition or current value.
- Consult your SIP plan rules and your employer's share scheme administrator for exact figures.
