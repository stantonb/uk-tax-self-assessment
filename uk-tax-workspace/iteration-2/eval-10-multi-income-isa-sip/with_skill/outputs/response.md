## Step 1: Your SIP Shares — Understand the Tax Before Acting

This is where careful attention is needed before we get to the income tax calculation, because the SIP situation directly affects your income figures.

### Free Shares (£3,600) — Held 3 Years

You are in the **3-to-5-year partial relief window** — this is the "SIP trap" that catches many employees off guard.

- Shares held **less than 3 years**: full market value at withdrawal taxed as employment income + NI.
- Shares held **3 to 5 years**: the **lower of** the market value at award or market value at withdrawal is taxed as employment income + NI. This is partial relief only — there is still a tax and NI charge.
- Shares held **5 years or more**: completely free of Income Tax and NI.

Since your free shares have been held for **3 years**, you are in the partial relief tier. The taxable amount is the lower of the value when awarded versus the value now. At the time of award, your employer contributed free shares up to the £3,600 annual limit. If the shares are still worth £3,600 or more, the taxable amount is £3,600 (the award value). If they have fallen in value, you are taxed on the current lower value.

**Key point**: Your employer should handle this through PAYE, and it should appear on your P11D or as an adjustment through payroll. You do not separately calculate and pay this yourself — check your payslip or P11D to confirm it has been processed correctly.

**Planning note**: If you have not yet withdrawn these shares, and you can wait another **2 years** to reach the 5-year mark, you would pay **zero Income Tax and NI** on them. That is a potentially significant saving. Unless you have a pressing reason to withdraw them now, staying put for 2 more years is strongly worth considering.

### Partnership Shares (£1,800)

You purchased these from **gross salary** — meaning you did not pay Income Tax or NI on the £1,800 used to buy them. This benefit has already been received. The same holding period rules apply for any future withdrawal. Since these are in the SIP alongside the free shares, track the holding period carefully.

**For this tax year's calculation**: Assuming the free shares are being included in your income as processed by PAYE (your employer handles this), we include £3,600 as employment income subject to income tax via PAYE. The partnership shares do not create additional income this year — the tax relief on purchase has already occurred. We will include the free shares value in the income calculation below.

## Step 7: Should You Be Moving More Into Your ISA?

**Yes — and this is one of the most straightforward tax planning decisions available to you.** Here is why:

### You Have £10,000 Remaining ISA Allowance This Year

The annual ISA allowance is £20,000. You have contributed £10,000, leaving **£10,000 of unused allowance** for 2024/25. The allowance does not carry forward — if you do not use it by 5 April 2025, it is gone permanently.

### The Tax Saving Is Real and Compounding

Every pound of investment income earned inside an ISA is completely tax-free. Looking at your current position:

- **Dividends**: You paid £979 tax on £3,400 of GIA dividends. If those same dividend-paying assets were inside your ISA, that £979 would be zero.
- **Future capital gains**: Your GIA gain this year (£2,800) was within the exempt amount, but the exempt amount has been sharply reduced in recent years (it was £12,300 in 2022/23 and is now £3,000). As your GIA portfolio grows, gains will increasingly exceed the exempt amount and be taxed at 24%.
- **Savings**: Any savings interest within a Cash ISA is also tax-free and does not erode your PSA.

### The Bed and ISA Strategy

If you hold shares in your GIA that have unrealised gains, consider a **bed and ISA** approach:

1. Sell the shares in your GIA — this crystallises a disposal for CGT.
2. Check the gain against your £3,000 annual exempt amount.
3. Immediately repurchase the same shares inside your Stocks and Shares ISA.

The 30-day anti-avoidance rule (bed and breakfast rule) does **not** apply when you rebuy inside an ISA. This means you can sell and immediately rebuy the same shares in your ISA on the same day without the disposal being matched to the ISA purchase.

Over time, this gradually moves your portfolio into the ISA wrapper, sheltering all future growth and income from tax entirely.

### Practical Priority

Given your tax position as a higher rate taxpayer:
- Every £1 of dividend income above your £500 allowance costs you 33.75p in tax.
- Every £1 of capital gains above £3,000 costs you 24p.
- Inside your ISA: both are zero.

**Recommendation**: Use as much of your remaining £10,000 ISA allowance as you can before 5 April 2025, prioritising assets that generate the most taxable income or gains in your GIA.
