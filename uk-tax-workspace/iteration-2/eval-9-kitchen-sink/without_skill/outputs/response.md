## Step 7: Rental Property — Mortgage Interest Restriction

This is where many landlords get caught out.

You mentioned:
- Rental profit after expenses: £6,200 (this presumably means after all expenses **except** mortgage interest)
- Mortgage interest: £4,800

Under the **Section 24 mortgage interest restriction** (fully phased in since 2020/21), you cannot deduct mortgage interest from rental income. Instead, you get a **20% tax credit** on the mortgage interest.

So your taxable rental income = £6,200 + £4,800 = **£11,000** gross rent minus non-interest expenses.

Wait — let me re-read your figures. You said "£6,200 profit after expenses, mortgage interest was £4,800 on top of that." This means:

- Net rental income (profit after all non-finance costs) = £6,200
- The £4,800 mortgage interest cannot be deducted from rental income — it gives a 20% credit instead

**Taxable rental income: £6,200** (the profit figure you have given is the correct taxable rental profit under current rules, assuming you have not already deducted the mortgage interest)

**Mortgage interest tax credit: £4,800 × 20% = £960** (this reduces your final tax bill)

If you calculated your £6,200 figure by deducting the mortgage interest, then your taxable rental income is actually £6,200 + £4,800 = £11,000, and you get a £960 credit against tax. The net effect is the same financially, but you must report the gross £11,000 on your Self Assessment.

Your ANI is £103,500 — just £3,500 above the £100,000 threshold. You are losing £1,750 of personal allowance, which at a 40% marginal rate costs you **£700** in extra tax.

To bring ANI to exactly £100,000, you need additional pension contributions of **£3,500**.

If you increase to bring ANI to £80,000 or below, you also **eliminate the Child Benefit charge** (worth £2,213/year).

To bring ANI from £103,500 to £80,000, you need £23,500 of additional pension contributions.

That is an effective relief rate of over 52% on those contributions, plus you build retirement wealth.

You can contribute additionally via:

### 5. Cryptocurrency — Keep Detailed Records
