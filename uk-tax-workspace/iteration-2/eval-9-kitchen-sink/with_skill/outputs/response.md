## Step 2: The £100,000 Personal Allowance Trap — This Is Your Biggest Issue

Your adjusted net income (ANI) is approximately **£103,500**. This puts you inside the personal allowance taper zone.

**How the taper works:**
- For every £2 of ANI above £100,000, you lose £1 of personal allowance.
- ANI of £103,500 is £3,500 above £100,000.
- Personal allowance lost: £3,500 / 2 = **£1,750**
- Remaining personal allowance: £12,570 - £1,750 = **£10,820**

**Why this is painful — the 60% effective marginal rate:**

In the £100,000–£125,140 band, each additional £1 of income costs you:
- 40p in higher rate income tax, PLUS
- 20p in lost tax-free allowance (which itself gets taxed at 40%)
- Total: effectively **60p in every £1** goes to HMRC

On the £3,500 above £100,000, you are paying approximately £2,100 in extra tax compared to what you would pay if your income were £100,000 exactly.

**What you can do about it:**

Making additional pension contributions (personal SIPP) or increasing salary sacrifice to bring ANI to exactly £100,000 restores the full personal allowance. The maths is compelling:

- You need to reduce ANI by approximately **£3,500**
- A £3,500 SIPP contribution costs you net roughly £1,400 after 40% relief AND the restored personal allowance effect — an effective tax saving significantly greater than 40%

This alone is one of the highest-value tax actions available to you this year.

## Step 4: Capital Gains Tax — Ethereum and Token Swaps

### The Ethereum disposal

You have a £7,200 gain from selling Ethereum.

**Annual exempt amount: £3,000**

Net chargeable gain: £7,200 - £3,000 = **£4,200**

**CGT rate:** Your total income of £103,500 already comfortably fills and exceeds the higher-rate threshold (£50,270). This means your Ethereum gain sits entirely in the higher-rate band.

**Important: the mid-year CGT rate change on 30 October 2024**

For 2024/25, CGT rates on assets (other than residential property) changed:
- Disposals before 30 October 2024: higher rate = **20%**
- Disposals on or after 30 October 2024: higher rate = **24%**

You need to identify when you sold the Ethereum:
- If before 30 October 2024: CGT = £4,200 x 20% = **£840**
- If on or after 30 October 2024: CGT = £4,200 x 24% = **£1,008**

### Token swaps — this is a critical point you must not overlook

You mention doing "a few token swaps." **Under HMRC rules, swapping one cryptoasset for another is a disposal for CGT purposes — even though you received no fiat currency.**

Each swap is a taxable event. For each swap you need to know:
- The GBP value of the tokens you disposed of at the time of the swap
- The cost basis of those tokens (using the same-day rule, 30-day rule, then Section 104 pool in that priority order)
- The gain or loss on each swap

If the token swaps produced gains, these must be added to your Ethereum gain and taxed accordingly. If they produced losses, they can be set against the gain (reducing or potentially eliminating your CGT bill — but only down to the level of the annual exempt amount in the current year; excess losses carry forward).

You also need to check whether your total disposal proceeds for the year exceed four times the annual exempt amount (4 x £3,000 = £12,000). If they do, you must complete the SA108 Capital Gains pages even if your net gains are below the exemption.

**Practical action:** Pull your exchange transaction history and calculate each swap separately. Use consistent GBP valuations from a reputable source at the time of each transaction.
